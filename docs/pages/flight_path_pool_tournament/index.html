Sunday Pool Tournament Rules:

Tournament Structure

The tournament is a bracket-style, double-elimination tournament. Players will be initally randomly paired with eachother to play in race-to-2 matches to advance, after which point winners proceed in the bracket, and losers join the redemption bracket.

At the end of the bracket, the winner of the winners bracket plays the winner of the redemption bracket.

In order to win the tournament, the winner-bracket player must win one match, whereas the redemption-bracket player must win two.

Breaking

May be decided by:
  • coin flip
  • lag for break
  • mutatual agreement
Note on lagging for break: After hitting the opposite rail, the ball which gets the closest to the the near rail WITHOUT HITTING IT wins.

Scratching

The cue ball must make contact with the object ball, THEN a ball must make it to either a pocket or a rail otherwise it's considered a scratch. If the cue ball goes off the table, it's also considered a scratch.

Ball-In-Hand

If a player scratches, the other player may place the cue ball anywhere they choose before making their shot.

The only exception to this rule is when a player has scratched on the break, in which case the other player plays from the kitchen.

When playing from the kitchen, a player must shoot from behind the second diamond (same side as the inital break) and may only target balls on the far side of the second diamond.

Call-Your-Pocket

Before shooting, players must name both their target ball (the ball they intend to pocket) and their destination (which pocket the target ball will go in).

If the player fails to pocket their target ball, play proceeds as normal to the next player.

8-Ball Rules

In order to win, a player's final shot must be to call and pocket the 8-ball.

If a player shooting on the 8-ball pockets the cue ball AND the other player still has balls on the table, it is treated as a table-scratch and play proceeds with the other player getting ball-in-hand.

If the 8-ball is pocketed in any pocket other than the one the shooter called, it's an automatic loss.

If the 8-ball is pocked early, it's an automatic loss.

Rule Disputes

If at any time players disagree about the rules, the tournament director will have final say. After the ruling, these rules will be clarified to reflect any updates.
